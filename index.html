About Me
⚔ Purple Team Mindset
Red informs blue. Blue sharpens red. My path into offensive security isn't about breaking things — it's about understanding how attacks work so the defenses I build actually hold. I've spent real time in live SIEM dashboards during peak attack windows, watching authentication anomalies and job failures in real time. Now I'm studying the other side of that equation. PenTest+ isn't just a certification — it's the formal foundation of a purple team philosophy I've been building since the first time I wrote a Sentinel detection rule and asked: "how would I have evaded this?"

I'm Kiarah Pettaway — Kay. IAM and security operations professional with hands-on experience across enterprise identity governance, front-line incident response, and regulated financial environments including Federal Reserve infrastructure, FFIEC, NCUA, FedRAMP, and ISO 27001. I hold CompTIA CySA+, SSCP, and Security+ — and I'm working toward PenTest+.

"I know exactly how the attack works. That's why the detection rule caught it."

My day job at SpartanNash has me triaging 30+ incidents daily with a 96% SLA resolution rate — spanning identity platforms, mainframe systems, POS infrastructure, and military access systems. Before that: a Security Engineer internship at Bindplane where I led ISO 27001 and FedRAMP documentation consolidation and deployed Python automation across 100+ config files. Before that: running live SIEM dashboards overnight at CU*Answers, processing Federal Reserve ACH transactions, and keeping $50M+ in daily financial operations stable.

I also received WGU's Systems Thinking Award in 2024 — because seeing how systems fail is a security skill, not just a business one.

Community: Active member of WGU Cyber Club, Women in CyberSecurity (WiCyS) Community Moderator, and Women in Technology (WIT). The mission: PenTest+. M.S. Cybersecurity (planned). Conferences. CTFs. A home lab running 24/7. I want to defend networks and think like the one trying to break in — then build the detections that catch them both.
