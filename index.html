About Me

I'm Kiarah Pettaway — Kay. I work in security operations and identity governance. Day to day, that means triaging incidents, managing access at enterprise scale, monitoring infrastructure in real time, and making sure the right people have the right access — and the wrong people don't.

I've operated in environments most people only read about. I've authenticated into Federal Reserve infrastructure to process ACH transactions. I've run live SIEM dashboards overnight monitoring 300+ financial institutions while the rest of the world slept. I've led IAM and access control reviews under ISO 27001 and FedRAMP frameworks. I've built Python automation that replaced hours of manual work. Right now I'm triaging 30+ incidents daily with a 96% SLA resolution rate across identity platforms, mainframes, POS systems, and military access environments.

I hold CompTIA CySA+, SSCP, Security+, Network+, A+, and ITIL — and I earned a WGU Systems Thinking Award in 2024 for applied systems modeling. I finished my B.S. in Cybersecurity & Information Assurance at WGU in 2026.

"I know what the attack looks like in logs. Now I'm learning to be the one making them."

Here's where I'm headed. I'm building toward offensive security — not to leave defense behind, but to make my defense sharper. PenTest+ is next on the cert track. A Raspberry Pi honeypot is live on my network collecting real attacker behavior right now. I'm writing IAM automation tooling in Python. I'm building IR playbooks from scratch. I'm planning my M.S. in Cybersecurity. I want to attend security conferences, contribute to the community, and keep stacking real skills — not just credentials.

The goal is simple: be the person who can defend a network, think like the one trying to break in, and build the detections that catch them both. That's the purple team loop — and I'm building it deliberately.

⚔ Purple Team Mindset
Red informs blue. Blue sharpens red. Every exploit I study becomes a detection rule. Every detection rule reveals a new evasion path to study. I spent real time in live SIEM dashboards during peak attack windows. Now I'm studying the other side of that equation — and bringing everything back to defense.
